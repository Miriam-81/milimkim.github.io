Teaching Method & Philosophy

1. Learner-centred teaching and learning tailored to developing practical communication skills

  • Beginner level: Based on a foundation course focusing on the Korean alphabet and phonics, building sentence structures to develop basic communication skills such as self-introduction, polite requests, asking questions, and describing weekend activities.
  • Intermediate level: Practising authentic communication through a variety of topics using compound and complex sentences.
  • Advanced level: Expanding vocabulary and grammar from everyday topics to current affairs and general knowledge.

2. Use of a variety of learner-engaging materials to support learning

Language input can be more effectively transformed into language acquisition when meaningful and authentic stimuli are integrated into the learning process. To facilitate this, vocabulary and sentence structures are selected in line with the learner’s communicative goals.

3. Integration of social and cultural aspects reflected in language

When learning a completely new language, understanding why it is used in a particular way within the society where it is spoken helps learners learn more easily and makes the learning process more interesting. During lessons, learners are also encouraged to explore the unique features of Korean within its social and cultural contexts.

Teaching Materials

Based on the learner’s level, the Sejong Korean textbook published by the National Institute of the Korean Language will be used as the main material. In addition, the tutor provides lesson notes for each chapter, including additional explanations and vocabulary lists, and short video clips for the lesson.
